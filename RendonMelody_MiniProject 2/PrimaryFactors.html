The Black Death was believed to have originated in Asia, but brought to the Western world during the port-siege of Crimea (Feodosiya, Ukraine). Mongolian ruler Jani Beg allowed his army to launch corpses contaminated with the plague over the cities. As trade continued on the port, the plague was carried hundreds of miles over the sea. The plague expanded from Ukraine westward towards Europe, affecting countries such as Italy, France, Germany, Spain, United Kingdom, and even Scandinavia. Mediterranean islands such as Mesopotamia and Syria were later affected.
